About:

Growing up, I was always fascinated with technology. Then, in high school I had the opportunity to learn the fundamentals of computer programming, starting with C++ and Java. After graduating, I decided to pursue a career in Information Technology, so I enrolled in a technical college. Over the next two years I earned my Associate degree in Computer Support and Networking.

In 2013, I was hired by the University of Georgia as the IT helpdesk manager for the Facilities Management Division. For the next two years I managed the physical computers, began automating processes with PowerShell, and learned how to maintain our layer-2 network and small VDI environment. Then, in 2015 I had the opportunity to move into system administration and continue refining my skills. Since then, I have built up the VDI environment that now services roughly 800 employees for our division, streamlined computer deployments and automated other tasks, managed the layer-2 network for our buildings, and deployed and maintained Palo Alto firewalls to protect our datacenters. Additionally, I spent two years working with Access Control supporting the access control, video monitoring, and related back-end systems for the university.

Currently, I am also working on obtaining my Bachelor of Information Technology online at Kennesaw State University. My current course work includes web development and hardware & software concepts. I have also completed courses covering Python, C#, and pre-calculus, and have a 4.0 Institutional GPA. My goal after graduation is to move into a higher leadership role as an IT Director leveraging my experience to guide and inspire a successful team.

When I'm not staring at firewall logs, optimizing VDI login times, or taking online classes, I enjoy dreaming of having the time to play video games. Speaking of games, would you like to play Rock, Paper, Scissors?
